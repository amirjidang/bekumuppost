Who is American Idol Season 18 Contestant Lauren Mascitti?

Biography Timeline

Lauren Mascitti is an American country singer and songwriter from Ohio who rose to prominence after winning the 18th season of the singing competition show American Idol. Here is a detailed biography timeline of Lauren Mascitti:
  • 1992: Lauren Mascitti was born in Canton, Ohio, United States.
  • 2014: She graduated from Belmont University in Nashville, Tennessee, with a degree in Songwriting.
  • 2018: Mascitti released her debut album, “Love Like This,” produced by Grammy-nominated musician Dave Moody.
  • 2020: She auditioned for the eighteenth season of American Idol in Savannah, Georgia, where she impressed the judges with her original song “If I Can Lose You.”
  • 2020: Mascitti made it to the Top 21 of American Idol, where she received high praise from the judges for her performances of songs such as “Two More Bottles of Wine” and “You Ain’t Woman Enough.”
  • 2020: She won the 18th season of American Idol, beating out fellow finalists Just Sam and Arthur Gunn.
  • 2020: Mascitti released her first single after American Idol, titled “God Made a Woman.”
  • 2021: She released her second single, “Keep It Simple,” which received positive reviews from critics and fans.
  • 2021: Mascitti got engaged to fellow musician Shawn Camp.
Lauren Mascitti’s journey to becoming a successful country singer has been a long and winding road.
