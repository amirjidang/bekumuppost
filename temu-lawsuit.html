Temu Lawsuit Alleges Intellectual Property Infringement and Forced Exclusivity

Temu has filed a lawsuit against Shein, accusing the rival online shopping platform of infringing on its intellectual property rights and engaging in forced exclusivity. The lawsuit alleges that Shein has unlawfully detained merchants, stolen their accounts and passwords, and coerced them to leave the Temu platform.

This alleged conduct by Shein is seen as an attempt to impede Temu’s growth in the US market. The lawsuit also cites previous allegations of copyright infringement made by both companies against each other, highlighting the contentious relationship between the two online retail giants.

Temu’s Allegations:

“Shein has falsely imprisoned our merchants and violated their rights by unlawfully detaining them. Additionally, the theft of merchant accounts and passwords has disrupted business operations and tarnished our reputation. Shein’s actions force merchants to leave our platform, undermining healthy competition and the freedom to choose.”

Impact on Temu’s Growth:

The allegations in the lawsuit suggest that Shein’s actions are aimed at hindering Temu’s expansion in the US market. By illegally detaining merchants and forcing them to leave, Shein intends to limit Temu’s access to a diverse range of products and suppliers, thereby hampering its ability to offer a wide selection of affordable goods to consumers.
