    Grace Phipps Career

    Acting

    Gillam moved to Los Angeles after graduating from high school in 2010 to pursue a career in acting. After only seven days, she landed the lead role of Bee in DreamWorks’ 2011 remake of the 1985 comedy horror film Fright Night.

    That same year, she debuted as Amy in the ABC Family fantasy drama series The Nine Lives of Chloe King. After one season, the show was canceled.

    Gillam was cast as April Young, a recurring character who plays an edgy natural beauty in The CW’s supernatural drama The Vampire Diaries, from 2012 to 2013, and appeared in 10 episodes of the fourth season.

    Gillam appeared in Teen Beach Movie, a Disney Channel Original Movie, in 2013 as cute biker girl Lela, doing an Annette Funicello impression, singing and dancing alongside Ross Lynch and Maia Mitchell.

    With 13.5 million viewers, the film was the second-highest-rated cable film in television history. Phipps had several featured solos, including “Falling for Ya.” In 2013, Gillam appeared in three episodes of the ABC TV comedy series Baby Daddy as Megan, Ben’s (Jean-Luc Bilodeau’s) love interest, in the fantasy-horror TV series Supernatural as Hael, and in the short The Signal as Zoe.

    The following year, Phipps appeared as diva movie star Brandy Braxton in the episode “Directors & Divas” of Disney’s teen sitcom Austin & Ally. Gillam co-starred and reprised her role as the fiercely independent and brassy biker chick Lela in Teen Beach 2, a sequel teen musical, alongside Ross Lynch, Maia Mitchell, Garrett Clayton, and John DeLuca in 2015. It was the most-watched cable movie of 2015, with 5.8 million viewers.

    Gillam also played Mona Wilson in the horror film Dark Summer, which was released in 2015. She also starred as Kaitlin, a troubled teen cheerleader, in the emotional horror/slasher film Some Kind of Hate, which premiered at the 2015 Stanley Film Festival, Fantasia International Film Festival, and FrightFest.

    She also played Mandy in three episodes of Fox’s black comedy-horror TV series Scream Queens that year, and Alice in Paul Solet’s punk rock revenge segment of horror-comedy Tales of Halloween. She also appeared as Vanessa Gillerman in the police procedural drama TV series CSI: Cyber and as Erica Young in the police procedural TV series Hawaii Five-0.

    She was also on the cover of Girls’ Life magazine’s June/July 2015 issue. She appeared on the Syfy TV series Z Nation as Sgt. Lilly from 2017 to 2018.

    Music

    The Teen Beach Movie soundtrack, which debuted at number eight on the Billboard 200 chart in July 2013, featured songs performed by Gillam, including her as a main artist in the song “Falling for Ya” and as a featured artist in the songs “Cruisin’ for a Bruisin’”, “Like Me”, “Meant to Be”, and “Coolest Cats in Town”.

    “Cruisin’ for a Bruisin’” and “Falling for Ya” spent weeks at the top of Billboard’s Kid Digital charts, with “Falling for Ya” peaking at number 25 on the Heatseekers Songs chart and number 66 on the Hot Digital Songs chart. Phipps performed “The Pilot and the Mermaid” with Ross Lynch in the Austin & Ally episode “Directors & Divas” in 2014.

    In 2015, her song “Gotta Be Me” from Teen Beach 2 was nominated for Best Song from a Movie or TV Show at the Teen Choice Awards; she performed the song with Ross Lynch, Maia Mitchell, Garrett Clayton, John Deluca, and Jordan Fisher.
