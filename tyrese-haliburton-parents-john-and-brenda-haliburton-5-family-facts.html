Tyrese Haliburton’s parents, John and Brenda Haliburton, have been with him since he started his basketball career at Oshkosh North High School. Currently, he competes for the National Basketball Association’s Indiana Pacers.

Tyrese established the program’s single-game assist record while a freshman at Iowa State. Despite suffering a wrist injury that ended his season, he enjoyed a breakthrough season as a sophomore & chosen to the second-team All-Big 12 Conference.

Similarly, Tyrese represented the USA at the 2019 FIBA Under-19 World Cup in Heraklion, Greece. He had 21 points on June 30 in a 102-84 group-stage victory over Lithuania while shooting 8 of 9 from the field.

Haliburton averaged 7.9 points, 6.9 assists & a 69 percent field goal percentage per game. He was selected for the tournament’s all-star squad & helped the United States win a gold medal.

He was selected for the Big 12 second team. He declared after the season that he give up his remaining collegiate basketball eligibility & enter the 2020 NBA draft.
