These TV Stars All Made A Salary Of Over $1 Million Per Episode

August 2024 · 8 minute read

It doesn't seem like a TV show could be as lucrative as a movie deal, but for some of Hollywood's elite, it can be even more than that. TV shows have proven to be lucrative ventures for actors and producers, as they possess the ability to generate a steady stream of income over numerous seasons. This continual success in the television industry allows these individuals to accumulate a significant amount of wealth over time.

Moreover, popular TV series often pave the way for spin-off productions, merchandise agreements, and syndication rights, all of which contribute to their lasting profitability. In fact, it is not uncommon for exceptional actors to receive staggering salaries of $1 million or more per episode. Albeit not exceedingly prevalent, there are indeed several noteworthy shows that boast casts who have achieved this extraordinary level of financial success—an astonishing revelation that may well surprise audiences.

15 Jennifer Aniston And Reese Witherspoon Made $2 Million Per Episode On The Morning Show

Jennifer Aniston and Reese Witherspoon earned significant salaries for their work on the Apple TV+ series The Morning Show. Jennifer Aniston, who played the role of Alex Levy, reportedly earned around $2 million per episode for her work on the show.

This made her one of the highest-paid actors on television at the time. Reese Witherspoon, who portrayed Bradley Jackson, also earned a substantial salary per episode. While the exact amount was not publicly disclosed, it was reported to be in the range of $2 million per episode as well. Both actresses not only starred in the series but also served as executive producers, contributing to the show's production and creative aspects.

14 Charlie Sheen Made $1.8 Million Per Episode On Two And A Half Men

Charlie Sheen made $1.8 million an episode on Two And A Half Men, which made him one of the highest-paid actors in television history. His portrayal of the womanizing jingle writer, Charlie Harper, on the hit sitcom was both beloved and controversial.

Sheen's larger-than-life personality and undeniable talent captivated audiences for eight seasons, propelling Two and a Half Men to immense success, like making $155 million in its last season. However, behind the scenes, Sheen's personal life was spiraling out of control, leading to his infamous public meltdown and subsequent departure from the show.

13 Ray Romano Made $1.7 Million Per Episode On Everyone Loves Raymond

Ray Romano was one of the first actors to garner $1 million per episode on a TV show. With his role as Ray on Everyone Loves Raymond, Romano not only earned $1.7 million per episode, he became a household name. His comedic talent was undeniable.

Audiences were captivated by his relatable portrayal of a loving, yet often exasperated husband and father. Romano's impeccable timing and natural delivery made his character endearing and hilarious, earning him numerous accolades and a dedicated fan base.

12 Kelsey Grammer Made $1.6 Million Per Episode On Frasier

Frasier starred Kelsey Grammar, and was a hit TV show. So much of a hit, Kelsey topped off at $1.6 million per episode! In addition to Kelsey Grammar's impressive salary, Frasier also garnered critical acclaim and a dedicated fan base.

The show, which aired for 11 seasons from 1993 to 2004, followed the life of Dr. Frasier Crane, a witty and neurotic psychiatrist turned radio host. With its clever writing, brilliant ensemble cast, and sophisticated humor, Frasier quickly became a cultural phenomenon, cementing its place in television history.

11 Chris Pratt Made $1.4 Million Per Episode On The Terminal List

The Terminal List is a TV show about a Navy SEAL named James Reece (Chris Pratt) who seeks revenge on those responsible for the death of his entire team during a covert mission. The show follows Reece as he uncovers a web of conspiracy and corruption within the highest levels of government, leading him on a dangerous journey to expose the truth and bring justice to those who betrayed him. For his role in the show, Chris Pratt made $1.4 million per episode, enough for a $15.6 million home, which he bought for his family.

10 Tim Allen Made $1.25 Million Per Episode On Home Improvement

On Home Improvement, Tim Allen worked his way up to $1.25 million per episode. That was a staggering amount for that time period, but the Tool Time show was such a huge success, it's no wonder why. The combination of Tim Allen's comedic talent and the relatable premise of a bumbling handyman Al resonated with audiences, making Home Improvement a beloved sitcom.

9 Steve Carrell Made $1 Million Per Episode On Space Force

Steve Carrell was also on The Morning Show but didn't quite hit the same salary level as Aniston and Witherspoon. He did, however, pull in $1 million per episode on Netflix's Space Force. The show, which premiered in 2020, garnered immense popularity, attracting a dedicated fanbase eagerly awaiting each new episode. Carrell's impressive paycheck not only reflected his talent and market value but also highlighted the streaming giant's commitment to producing high-quality content.

8 The Entire Cast Of Friends Each Made $1 Million Per Episode

During the peak popularity of the show Friends, the cast members, including Jennifer Aniston, Courteney Cox, Lisa Kudrow, Matt LeBlanc, Matthew Perry, and David Schwimmer, were some of the highest-paid actors in the industry.

Their $1 million per episode salary was a historic milestone in television and made headlines at the time. This significant income solidified their status as highly successful actors and highlighted the immense success and popularity of the show.

7 The Entire Cast Of The Big Bang Theory Each Made $1 Million Per Episode

The Big Bang Theory cast members had their salaries increased near the end, and it wound up with all of them making $1 million per episode. This substantial increase in salaries for The Big Bang Theory cast members not only reflected the immense popularity and success of the show, but it also highlighted the pivotal roles each actor played in its enduring appeal.

6 Nicole Kidman And Reese Witherspoon Made $1 Million Per Episode On Big Little Lies

Nicole Kidman and Reese Witherspoon each pulled in $1 million per episode on Pretty Little Lies. This impressive paycheck is a testament to the incredible talent and star power that both actresses brought to the critically acclaimed series. Their portrayal of complex, multi-dimensional characters captivated audiences worldwide. 5 Jerry Seinfeld Made $1 Million Per Episode On Seinfeld

Jerry Seinfeld is another member of the $1 million per episode club. His time on Seinfeld was very lucrative for him, as the show became one of the most successful sitcoms in television history. Seinfeld's comedic talent and wit, combined with the popularity of his character, contributed to his immense earning power during his time on the show.

4 Helen Hunt And Paul Riser Made $1 Million Per Episode On Mad About You

Astonishingly, Paul Riser and Helen Hunt made $1 million per episode in the hit show Mad About You. Again, for its time period, that was a tremendous amount of money. The show was about a married couple navigating the ups and downs of their relationship in New York City.

It was praised for its relatable humor and realistic portrayal of married life. The chemistry between Riser and Hunt was a major factor in the show's success, captivating audiences and keeping them coming back for more each week.

3 Five Of The Main Cast Members On Game Of Thrones Made $1 Million Per Episode

Tyrion Lannister, Jaime Lannister, Cersie Lannister, Daenerys Tagaryen, and Jon Snow all hit $1 million per episode on Game of Thrones. The HBO hit show had the majority of the country tuning in each week, not able to get enough of the epic battles, political intrigue, and complex characters. With its gripping storytelling and stunning visuals, Game of Thrones became a cultural phenomenon that captivated viewers, and even produced a spinoff, House of Dragon.

2 James Gandolfini Made $1 Million Per Episode On The Sopranos

James Gandolfini pulled in $1 million per episode near the end of The Sopranos. He played the character of Tony Soprano, a complex and compelling mob boss.

The Sopranos was similar to a cult classic for most people; they became immersed in the mafia world and the intricate web of relationships within Tony Soprano's family and criminal organization. Gandolfini's portrayal of Tony Soprano was widely praised for its authenticity and depth, earning him three Emmy Awards for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series.

1 Jeff Bridges Made $1 Million Per Episode On Old Man

The Old Man is a new series that stars Jeff Bridges and is already a major hit! The series follows Dan Chase (Jeff Bridges), a former CIA operative and Vietnam veteran who has been living off the grid in Vermont for 30 years. His peaceful existence crumbled when an intruder broke into his home. In a split-second decision to protect himself, Chase took the intruder's life. The weight of this action pushed him into hiding.
