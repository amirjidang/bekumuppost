Stephanie Mills Biography

Stephanie Mills (Stephanie Dorthea Mills) is an American singer, songwriter, and actress. Mills is a Grammy Award-winning R&B singer who starred as Dorothy in the original Broadway run of the musical The Wiz from 1975 to 1977. She has released hit singles like “Never Knew Love Like This Before,” which peaked at #6 on the Billboard Top 100.

Mills honed her vocals as a singer at the Brooklyn Cornerstone Baptist church at a tender age. At the age of nine years, Mills began appearing at the broadway show Maggie Flynn.

Stephanie Mills Parents

Mills was born in Brooklyn, New York to Joseph Mills and Christine Mills in Bedford–Stuyvesant, Brooklyn. Mills sang gospel music as a child at Brooklyn’s Cornerstone Baptist Church. The musician commenced her professional profession when she was nine years old, appearing in the Broadway musical Maggie Flynn.

She was raised in Bedford Stuyvesant Brooklyn with her family. At 11, Mills won Apollo Theater Amateur Night for six consecutive weeks which led her to open for the Isley brothers. She signed a contract deal at 16 and ser her hit single I knew it was love in 1973.

Stephanie Mills In The Wiz

Mills’s career rose when she portrayed Dorothy in the Broadway musical The Wiz, an African-American adoption of The Wonderful Wizard of Oz. Her song “Home” from the show became her trademark song for years and was covered later by Diana Ross for the movie adaptation three years later.

Commercial achievement in the music industry remained difficult until 1979 when she signed to the 20th Century Fox Records label. She once again starred in the role of Dorothy in “The Wiz” in 1993 which closed after 28 performances.

Stephanie Mills Michael Jackson

The singer made headlines in 2018 August with her response to musician Sam Smith, who commented in a video posted on Instagram that he did not like Michael Jackson, but the Jackson hit “Human Nature” was a “decent song”. Mills’ fiery reply got traction as she scrutinized Smith in her own Instagram responses, both citing him of cultural appropriation and referring to him as a “one-hit wonder”.

Stephanie Mills Home

Home is the 12th studio album by Stephanie Mills. It was released on June 26, 1989, on MCA Records and peaked at no. 5 on Billboard Top R&B Albums. Top hits from the album were the singles “Home” and Something in the Way (You Make Me Feel)
