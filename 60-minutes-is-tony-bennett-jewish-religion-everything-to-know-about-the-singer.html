Is Tony Bennett Jewish? Learn about the famous American singer, as he prepares for the last big concert.

Tony is a professional singer, mainly known for traditional pop, jazz, big band, and show tunes. He has devoted most of his life to the singing career. 

After all those years of singing, Tony and Lady Gaga prepare for Bennett’s last concert. With his final act, he invited 60 minutes crew to get ready.

His fan followers have been more than happy to witness his last concert and preparation. Moreover, he has once again become the topic of public interest.

Is Tony Bennett Jewish? Religion Explored

Tony was born on 3 August 1926 to his Italian parents, Anna Suraci and John Benedetto. He grew up in New York, USA, and his father was from Podargoni, Calabria, Italy. 

His parents emigrated to the USA from Calabria, and that’s when they gave birth to the legend. Bennett is a Christian, and he has spoken a few things about his Christian faith. 

Tony, a professional American singer and a big heartthrob of his followers, is not Jewish. However, his daughter Antonia converted to Judaism and married her Israeli husband. 

Come Across Tony Bennett Wife Susan Crow & His Kids 

Tony married his third wife, Susan Crow, in 2007 after his relationship ended with Sandra Grant, his second wife, and Patricia Beech, his first wife. 

Bennett and Crow have been in a relationship for a long term. They started having a love affair in the late 1980s and eventually married. 

Susan is 40 years younger than Tony, who is 95-years-old now. The couple does not share any children, even after their long-lasting marriage. 

However, Bennett has four children from his past marriages, Danny and Dae from his first wife & Joanna and Antonia from his second wife. 

What Happened To Tony Bennett?

Tony has been battling Alzheimer’s disease since 2016. He opened up about his illness to the AARP magazine in February 2021.

Although with an illness, Bennett has been doing great in his singing career. As per his neurologist, Tony’s tour schedule has helped him with his disease to an extent. 

Nevertheless, Tony got retired from his singing and music career in August of 2021. He is now looking for his last concert and prepares for it while having his time with a 60-minutes crew.

A living legend with 19 Grammy Awards, and two Primetime Emmy Awards, is preparing for his last concert, and people have been more than thrilled about it.
