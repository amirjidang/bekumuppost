Elizabeth Berkley was one of the most beloved teen stars on TV before her transition to the big screen. What is Elizabeth Berkley famous for? The coming-of-age series gave Berkley a big break on TV, but her popularity significantly dwindled years after the show ended. Berkley's reputation in Hollywood was marred mainly by what was supposed to be her next big role in 1995's Showgirls.

When Saved By The Bell ended in 1993, Elizabeth Berkley tried to transition onto the big screen through the erotic thriller Showgirls. What Is Elizabeth Berkley Doing Years After Saved by the Bell?

Showgirls was a disappointment, but Elizabeth Berkley barely let it take over her life. She picked herself up again and approached acting with another style. The actress told People, "It felt right to make it more about the people I was working with and doing different kinds of roles to bring it back." She starred in minor roles in critically acclaimed movies like First Wives Club, The Curse of the Jade Scorpion, The Real Blonde, and Random Encounters.

The actress has yet to top her fame from Saved by the Bell, but her finances are not terrible. Like her cast mates, Berkley did not receive royalties from the series, but her finances did not suffer. Besides taking more roles in film and TV, she became an entrepreneur. She created Ask Elizabeth, a platform dedicated to empowering young girls. Contrary to fans' speculation, Elizabeth Berkley is nowhere near the poverty line. The actress has an impressive net worth through her acting career and business endeavors.

The TV star was inspired to create her Ask Elizabeth online resource for adolescents. This was followed by a self-help book in 2011. According to Screenrant, Elizabeth Berkley's last known film role was in 2009's Donnie Darko. Her TV catalog expanded with series like NYPD Blue, CSI: Crime Scene Investigation, The L Word, New Girl, The Twilight Zone, and Law & Order, among others.

What Happened To Elizabeth Berkley?

Starting as a model, Elizabeth Berkley was signed to Elite Model Management. In 1986, she appeared in a minor role in her first movie, Gimme a Break. The star's next role was in the 1987 comedy-drama movie Frog. Berkley had minor roles until she auditioned for the role of Kelly Kapowski in Saved by the Bell in 1989. However, the role went to fellow star Tiffani-Amber Thiessen, which was a tough decision for the producers. Despite opting for Thiessen, the showrunners loved Berkley's acting, so they created the role of Jessie Spano for her.

From there, her career kicked off, as she played the recurring role from 1989 until the show ended in 1993. Elizabeth Berkley reprised her role in two made-for-TV films, Saved by the Bell: Hawaiian Style and Saved By the Bell: Wedding in Las Vegas. After Berkley's stint on the TV franchise, she crossed to the big screen by playing a stripper, Nomi Malone, in Paul Verhoeven's Showgirls. However, this role ended up hurting Berkley's career.

Showgirls caused controversies due to its sensual theme, and the actress was at the center of it all. Elizabeth Berkley went from being one of America's most beloved teen sweethearts to an actress scorned for her erotic role. The movie earned an NC-17 rating and flopped at the box office. However, the young actress continued to strive for more film roles. Speaking to People in 2020, she opened up about the downside of making Showgirls and its effects on her. Berkley revealed:

"It changed me. It was a life moment, and I cannot help but say I would be a different person had I not gone through the depths of what it taught me."
