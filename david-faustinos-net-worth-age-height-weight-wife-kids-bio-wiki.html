Early Life

David Faustino was born in Los Angeles, literally the hub of American entertainment, to Roger and Kay Faustino. The entertainment world beckoned David to become an actor, which he did when he appeared on the ‘Lily Tomlin Special’ aged barely three months old, too young even to understand the depth of the word.

However, it wasn’t until 1980 that his career as a full-time actor took off with minor roles in television shows, like ‘Little House on the Prairie,’ ‘St. Elsewhere’ and ‘The Love Boat’ to name a few before he eventually landed his breakthrough role of Bud Bundy in 1987.

Personal Life

On the personal front, David Faustino has two siblings, an older half-brother named Jeff Leiber and a younger brother named Michael Faustino, both of whom are actors. He has been married twice. Career

After a series of guest appearances in various television shows, David Faustino’s life changed in the year 1987 when he bagged his breakthrough role of Bud Bundy, which he went on to portray for the next ten years. His growing popularity with each successive season of the show brought him the opportunity to guest star in many other successful shows on television and in many of them, like ‘Parker Lewis Can’t Lose’ and ‘Top of the Heap’ he ended up reprising his most appreciated role of Bud Bundy.

Over the course of his illustrious career that spans almost 38 years, David Faustino has been a part of television shows, like ‘The New Addams Family,’ ‘One on One,’ ‘Entourage,’ ‘Bones,’ ‘Trapper John, M.D.,’ ‘Fantasy Island,’ ‘The Love Boat,’ ‘I Had Three Wives,’ ‘The Young and the Restless’ among others. He also participated in the popular reality show ‘Celebrity Boot Camp,’ a military training camp for celebrities.

Along with a venerated television career, David Faustino also went on to enjoy a rewarding film career with films, like ‘Killer Bud,’ ‘RoboDoc,’ ‘Not Another B Movie,’ ‘I’m Naked Thinking of You,’ ‘Perfect Harmony,’ ‘Kiss & Tell,’ ‘The Heist’ and ‘I Know That Voice’ under his belt.

Despite tasting much success as an actor, David Faustino couldn’t shake off his childhood dream of becoming a musician and continued to dabble with music, eventually coming out with his debut rap album ‘Balistyx’ under the stage name of D’ Lil. He also collaborated with popular musician Jon Dishon on his 2008 electronica album ‘Hollowdreams,’ penning down various songs and even producing the album.

Among David Faustino’s many talents is voice acting with various voice characters in much loved animated series, like ‘The Legend of Korra’ and ‘DreamWorks Dragons,’ a computer-generated animated television series spawned to bridge the story between the 2010 film ‘How to Train Your Dragon’ and its 2014 sequel. The two seasons of the show that came in between the release of the aforesaid two movies had David Faustino voicing Dagur the Deranged the supporting and main villain of the 1st and 2nd seasons, respectively.

David Faustino’s comic timing was at its utmost glory in the over the internet comedy series called ‘Starving,’ a project very close to his heart as he not only starred in it but also was its key developer. After the success of ‘Star-ving,’ he became a part of well-received web series, like ‘Dark Christmas’ and ‘Bad Samaritans.’
